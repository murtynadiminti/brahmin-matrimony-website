Brahmin Marriage Rituals

Brahmin weddings are deeply rooted in Vedic traditions. Here are the key rituals observed:

1. Nischitaardham (Engagement)

A formal agreement between families that includes exchanging horoscopes, gifts, and fixing the wedding date with blessings of elders and priests.

2. Snaatakam

Conducted on the day of wedding, groom and his parents perform this ritual and it marks the beginning of the sacred marriage ceremonies.

3. Kasi Yatra

The groom symbolically renounces worldly life and heads to Kashi with a walking stick. The bride's father intercepts him and convinces him to accept marriage instead, offering his daughter in marriage.

5. Mangalya Dharanam

The groom ties the mangalsutra (sacred thread) around the bride‚Äôs neck in three knots, symbolizing unity of mind, body, and soul.

6. Saptapadi

The couple takes seven steps around the sacred fire. Each step signifies a vow including nourishment, strength, prosperity, family, progeny, health, and friendship.

7. Talambralu

The bride and groom pour turmeric-coated rice over each other's heads. It represents joy, prosperity, and happiness in their married life.

8. Arundhati Darshanam

The couple is shown the Arundhati and Vasishta stars, signifying eternal loyalty and unity in marriage.

9. Gruhapravesam

The bride enters the groom's house and is welcomed with aarti. She kicks a pot of rice at the entrance to signify prosperity entering the household.
